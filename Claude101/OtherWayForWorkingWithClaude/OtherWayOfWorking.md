## 🧠 Lesson 1 — Your First Conversation with Claude

### 🎯 The 3 Elements of a Great Prompt

| Element | Question to Ask Yourself | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Setting the Stage** | What's my role & context? | *"I'm a marketing lead at a startup..."* |
| **Defining the Task** | What action do I want? | *"...research the streaming market..."* |
| **Specifying Rules** | What style/format do I need? | *"...structured as a professional report."* |

### 💡 The "Coworker Approach"
Talk to Claude **naturally, concisely, and conversationally** — like messaging a smart coworker, not typing a Google search.

### 🔄 Iterating on Responses
- Ask for more detail → *"Can you expand on point 2?"*
- Give feedback → *"Good, but make it more casual."*
- Redirect → *"Actually, I meant X, not Y."*

## 🎯 Lesson 2 — Getting Better Results

### 🐛 Common Challenges & How to Fix Them

| Problem | Why It Happens | The Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Too generic** | Not enough context | Add your role, audience & constraints |
| **Too long / too short** | Claude is guessing at length | Be explicit: *"Keep this under 100 words"* |
| **Wrong format** | Claude knows WHAT but not HOW | Show an example or describe the structure |
| **Wrong information** | Claude can occasionally be wrong | Verify facts independently for high-stakes work |
| **Wrong tone** | Claude defaults to professional | Say exactly what you want: *"More conversational"* |

### 🔁 The Iteration Mindset
> *"Your first prompt is the start of a conversation, not a one-shot request."*

✅ Treat first drafts as **starting points**  
✅ Give **specific feedback** → *"Cut the first two paragraphs"* not just *"Make it shorter"*  
✅ Know when to **start fresh** — sometimes a new chat is faster  

### 🏆 The 4D Framework for AI Fluency

| Competency | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 🎯 **Delegation** | Decide what AI does vs. what humans do |
| 📝 **Description** | Communicate clearly with AI |
| 🔍 **Discernment** | Critically evaluate AI outputs |
| 🛡️ **Diligence** | Use AI responsibly & ethically |

## 📁 Lesson 4 — Introduction to Projects

### What is a Project?
> A **Project** is a self-contained workspace with its own memory, chat history, knowledge base, and custom instructions — like a dedicated office for each area of your work.

### 🤔 When Should I Create a Project?

| Signal | Example |
|---|---|
| 📄 **Repeated reference materials** | Brand guidelines, meeting notes, reports |
| 📋 **Consistent requirements** | Always cite sources, always use our template |
| 👥 **Team collaboration needs** | Multiple people need the same shared context |

### 🚀 How to Create Your First Project — 3 Steps

**Step 1: Set Up Your Project**
1. Go to `claude.ai/projects` → click **"+ New Project"**
2. Give it a descriptive name → *"Q4 Marketing Campaign"*
3. Add a brief description
4. Choose visibility: **Private** or **Share with organization**

**Step 2: Write Project Instructions**
```
✅ Context    → "This project is for B2B software marketing content."
✅ Process    → "First draft a structure, then write the full piece."
✅ Tone       → "Professional but conversational. Avoid jargon."
✅ Rules      → "Always end marketing copy with a call-to-action."
```

**Step 3: Build Your Knowledge Base**
- 📘 Brand guidelines & style guides
- 📊 Research reports & meeting notes
- 📝 Templates & examples of great work
- 🔧 Technical docs & specifications

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Name files descriptively! `Q4-2024-Brand-Guidelines.pdf` helps Claude — `document1.pdf` doesn't.

### 🧠 RAG — How Projects Handle Large Knowledge Bases
```
Normal Mode  →  Claude loads ALL project content into memory
RAG Mode     →  Claude intelligently retrieves ONLY what's relevant
```
- 🔼 Expands capacity by up to **10x** — quality stays the same

### 👥 Collaboration & Permission Levels

## 🛠️ Lesson 6 — Working with Skills

### What is a Skill?
> A **Skill** is a folder of instructions, scripts, and resources that Claude loads automatically to perform specialized tasks in a **repeatable, consistent way** — like an expertise package.

You've already seen Skills in action whenever Claude creates Excel, PowerPoint, Word, or PDF files!

---

### 📦 Two Types of Skills

| Type | Created By | How It Works |
|---|---|---|
| 🔵 **Anthropic Skills** | Anthropic | Built-in, auto-invoked, available to all paid users |
| 🟣 **Custom Skills** | You / Your org | Tailored to your workflows, uploaded by you |

**Anthropic's built-in Skills include:**
- 📊 Excel spreadsheet creation
- 📝 Word document creation
- 📊 PowerPoint presentation creation
- 📄 PDF generation

---

### ⚙️ How to Enable Skills

1. Go to **Settings → Capabilities**
2. Toggle on **"Code execution and file creation"** *(Skills require this)*
3. Scroll to the **Skills section**
4. Toggle individual skills **on or off** as needed

> ⚠️ **Enterprise:** Owners must enable Skills in Admin settings first  
> ✅ **Team plans:** Enabled by default at the organization level

---

### 💬 Example Prompts That Invoke Skills

```
"Create an Excel spreadsheet tracking monthly expenses with totals"
"Turn these meeting notes into a PowerPoint presentation"
"Generate a PDF report summarizing this data"
"Build a financial model in Excel with scenario analysis"
```
When Claude uses a Skill, you'll see it mentioned in its chain of thought — and the output will be a **downloadable file** you can save or send to Google Drive.

---

### 🧰 Creating Your Own Custom Skill — 5 Steps

1. **Start a chat** → Tell Claude what skill you want to create
   - *"I want a skill for writing quarterly business reviews"*
2. **Answer Claude's questions** → It will interview you about your workflow
3. **Upload reference materials** → Templates, style guides, brand assets, examples
4. **Download your skill** → Claude generates a ZIP file with the skill structure
5. **Upload to Settings** → Settings → Capabilities → Skills → "Upload skill"

From that point on, Claude **automatically invokes it** whenever relevant — no manual triggering needed!

---

### 🆚 Skills vs. Projects — What's the Difference?

| | 📁 Projects | 🛠️ Skills |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Store **knowledge** Claude references | Define **processes** Claude executes |
| **Think of it as...** | A filing cabinet | A recipe book |
| **Best for** | Long-term context, reference materials | Repeatable workflows, consistent methodology |
| **Example** | Customer hub, research library | Brand guidelines applier, blog drafter |
| **Persistence** | Available across all chats in the project | Applied whenever the skill is invoked |

> 💡 **They work together!** A skill can pull knowledge stored in a project — the project provides the **WHAT** (information), the skill provides the **HOW** (process).

---

### 🔒 Security Considerations

- ✅ Only install custom Skills from **trusted sources**
- ✅ Anthropic's built-in Skills are **tested and maintained** by Anthropic
- ✅ Your custom Skills are **private** to your individual account
- ⚠️ Always **review contents** of external Skills before installing

## 🔌 Lesson 7 — Connecting Your Tools

### What are Connectors?
> **Connectors** transform Claude from a chat assistant into an **informed collaborator** — giving it access to the same tools, data, and context you use every day. No more copy-pasting information into every conversation!

---

### ⚡ What Can Connectors Do?

Depending on the connector and permissions you grant, Claude can:

| Action | Example |
|---|---|
| 🔍 **Search your files** | Find a doc in Google Drive |
| 📖 **Retrieve documents** | Pull up last week's meeting notes |
| 📊 **Analyze data** | Review Stripe revenue trends |
| ✍️ **Create new content** | Draft a Notion page |
| 🔄 **Update records** | Create a task in Asana |
| ✅ **Execute tasks** | Reply to a Slack message |

---

### 🔧 The Technology Behind It — MCP

> **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** = the universal standard that powers all connectors

Think of it like **USB-C for AI** — one standard plug that works with any tool. Developers can build a connector for any app, and it works seamlessly with Claude.

---

### 📦 Two Types of Connectors

| Type | What It Connects | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 🌐 **Web Connectors** | Cloud services | Gmail, Google Drive, Notion, Slack, Asana, Stripe |
| 🖥️ **Desktop Extensions** | Local apps & files | Local file access, browser control, Figma |

> ⚠️ Desktop extensions require the **Claude Desktop app** — they don't work in the browser

---

### 🚀 How to Set Up a Web Connector — 5 Steps

1. Go to **`claude.ai/directory`** or click **"Search and tools" → "Add connectors"**
2. Click **Connect** on the connector you want
3. **Authenticate** — sign in with your existing account credentials
4. **Grant permissions** — review exactly what Claude is asking for
5. **Test it** → *"Can you access my [tool name]?"*

## 🔎 Lesson 8 — Enterprise Search

### What is Enterprise Search?
> **Enterprise Search** adds a dedicated **"Ask {Your Org Name}"** button to your sidebar — a powerful tool specifically built for finding and synthesizing knowledge buried across ALL of your company's tools at once.

Unlike regular chat with connectors, Enterprise Search is **purpose-built for information gathering**, with custom instructions optimized by Anthropic for organizational knowledge.

---

### 💬 What Can You Ask?

**🚀 Getting Up to Speed**
```
"What happened yesterday while I was out?"
"Summarize key business updates from the last week"
"What are the current blockers on the Platform project?"
```

**📋 Policy & Process Questions**
```
"What is our company's remote work policy?"
"How do I submit an expense report?"
"What's the process for requesting time off?"
```

**🔬 Research & Analysis**
```
"What are the main reasons customers choose competitors?"
"Summarize discussions about the Q4 product roadmap"
"Find info about our customer onboarding process"
```

**🧑‍💼 Onboarding New Team Members**
```
"How does our authentication system work?"
"Who should I talk to about the billing system?"
"What tools does engineering use for deployment?"
```

**📊 Performance & Project Tracking**
```
"Find documents related to the marketing campaign"
"What were key decisions from last week's leadership meetings?"
"Summarize team contributions to the Infrastructure initiative"
```

> ✅ Claude always **cites its sources** so you can get the full context behind every answer!

---

### ⚙️ How to Set It Up

Enterprise Search needs **two steps**: admin setup first, then each user connects their own accounts.

**Step 1 — For Admins (Owners):**

1. Click **"Ask Your Org"** in the left sidebar
2. Click **"Set up for your org"**
3. Connect your organization's tools:
   - 📄 **Documents** *(required)* → Google Drive or SharePoint
   - 💬 **Chat** *(required)* → Slack or Microsoft Teams
   - 📧 **Email** *(recommended)* → Gmail or Outlook
4. Click **"+ Add more"** for any additional tools
5. Customize the project name → appears as *"Ask [Name]"* for everyone
6. Add a description → click **"Finish set up"**

**Step 2 — For Users:**

1. Click the **"Ask {Org Name}"** project in your sidebar
2. Follow the guided onboarding flow
3. Authenticate with each service (Slack, Google, Microsoft 365...)
4. Start asking questions!

> 💡 The more connectors you enable, the more comprehensive your search results will be.

---

### 🔒 Is All That Data Safe?

| Concern | The Answer |
|---|---|
| 🔐 **Can Claude see everything?** | No — it only shows data you already have permission to access |
| 💬 **Are my conversations private?** | Yes — conversations remain private |
| 🗃️ **Is my data stored separately?** | No — connected data is NOT indexed or stored elsewhere |

> **Simple rule:** Claude sees exactly what *you* see — nothing more, nothing less.

## 🔬 Lesson 9 — Research Mode for Deep Dives

### What is Research Mode?
> **Research** transforms Claude from a conversational assistant into a **systematic investigator** — conducting multiple searches that build on each other, exploring different angles automatically, and delivering a comprehensive report with citations in minutes instead of hours.

---

### ⚡ Key Facts

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| ⏱️ **How long does it take?** | 5–15 minutes for most reports, up to 45 min for complex ones |
| 🔗 **Citations included?** | Yes — every claim links back to its source |
| 🧠 **Extended thinking?** | Automatically enabled when Research is active |
| 🔍 **Web search required?** | Yes — must be enabled in Search & tools settings |

---

### 🤔 When Should I Use Research?

| Use **Research** when you need... | Use **Web Search** instead when... | Use **Extended Thinking** instead when... |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive multi-source reports | A quick specific fact | Deep reasoning with no external info needed |
| In-depth analysis & comparisons | Only 1–2 sources needed | Math, code debugging, logical analysis |
| Hours of work done in minutes | Speed > comprehensiveness | The answer comes from reasoning, not gathering |
| Verified citations | | |

---

### ⚙️ How Research Works — 4 Steps

```
Step 1: PLAN  →  Extended thinking activates — Claude maps out what to investigate
Step 2: SEARCH →  Claude runs many searches, each building on the last
Step 3: SYNTHESIZE → Claude compiles findings from web + your connected tools
Step 4: CITE  →  Every claim gets a source link for easy verification
```

---

### 🚀 How to Enable Research

1. Find the **Research button** on the bottom left of your chat
2. Click it — it turns **blue** when active
3. Write your prompt and submit
4. Watch progress indicators as Claude works in the background

> ⚠️ **Web search must be enabled** first in your Search and tools settings!

---

### ✍️ Tips for Effective Research Prompts

**❌ Too vague:**
```
"Tell me about the EV market"
```

**✅ Specific & structured:**
```
"Analyze the electric vehicle battery market — identify key players, 
technology trends, and supply chain challenges that might affect 
investment decisions."
```

**More pro tips:**
- 📐 **Specify structure** → *"Compare venues including: location, amenities, catering, and pricing"*
- 🎯 **Add constraints** → Budget, timeline, geography help Claude focus on what's relevant
- 🤝 **Ask Claude to help** → *"Help me write a better Research prompt for this question"* before enabling Research

---

### 🔗 Research + Connected Integrations = Super Power

When Google Workspace or other tools are connected, Research pulls from BOTH the web AND your personal data:

```
"Summarize what's been discussed about Project X across my emails 
and Slack, then research industry best practices for similar initiatives"

"Review my calendar for next week and research each company I'm meeting with"

"Find all internal docs about our pricing strategy and compare 
to how competitors are positioning themselves"
```

> 💡 Steer Claude with: *"Pull relevant context from my Google Drive"* or *"Include insights from my recent emails on this topic"*
